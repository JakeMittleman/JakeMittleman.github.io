What to Expect During the Ceremony
A Crash Course in Jewish Weddings
Kippah

The traditional Jewish head covering. Optional and kosher for all!

Chuppah

The wedding canopy that symbolizes the home the bride and groom will build together. It is open on all sides to represent the openness to family, friends, and community.

Shehecheyanu

A blessing that gives thanks to God for enabling us to experience a new or special occasion

Baruch atah, Adonai Eloheinu, Melech haolam, shehecheyanu, v'kiy'manu, v'higiyanu laz'man hazeh

Birkat Erusin

Betrothal blessing said over a glass of wine shared by the bride and groom

Exchange of the Rings

The exchanging of wedding bands is a sign of commitment to one another. Traditionally, these rings are unembellished and placed on the right index finger, which is believed to be connected by a special line directly to the heart.

Sheva Brachot

The Seven Wedding Blessings
These blessings are recited in the themes of creation of the world and humankind; importance of marriage; joy, love, and peace.

Ketubah

This is the Jewish marriage contract that is signed before the ceremony. It represents the bride and groom's commitment to each other.

Birkat Kohanim

The Priestly Benediction is the final blessing bestowed upon the wedding couple. During the blessing, the couple are wrapped in a tallit, a jewish prayer shawl, symbolizing their union.

Breaking of the Glass

The ceremony concludes with the groom and bride smashing a glass under their foot. After they smash the glass it is customary to clap and yell "Mazel Tov" which means "congratulations"!

Yichud

In hebrew, yichud means "one" and represents the transition when two individuals become one. After the ceremony, the couple goes to a room for a few minutes of privacy. This is a period of bonding and reflection before the public celebration.

Seudat Mitzvah

Guests at a Jewish wedding are commanded to share in the joy of the bride and groom. The seudat mitzvah is a celebratory feast so please join us as we continue to celebrate their marriage into the evening. Stick around after dinner to enjoy our entertainment for the evening!
